About Us

What the software does

Misinformation (false rumors or unverified information) is a powerful, pervasive, and persistent force that affects people and groups. In one of their recent studies, Stanford University provides the following definition for rumors: “We define rumors to be posts that are intentionally and verifiably false, and could mislead readers”. False rumors or unverified information can spread more rapidly and widely because individuals receiving information through this medium lack the capacity to determine the veracity of the information they receive. Social media and the internet is suffering from fake accounts, fake posts, and fake news. The intention is often to mislead readers and/or manipulate them into purchasing or believing something that isn’t real. In this project, our main motivation is to find out whether a post is fake or not. A comprehensive set of users, structural, linguistic, and temporal features will be examined. We will propose & design a rumor classification algorithm, which is primarily based on the pre-processing over real-time data and identifies probable reasons. The algorithm uses, user and linguistic features readily available and act as a good indicator during the initial propagation phase.
